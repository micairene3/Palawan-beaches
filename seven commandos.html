Seven Commandos Beach

Seven Commandos Beach in El Nido, Palawan, is a hidden gem known for its pristine white sand, turquoise waters, and lush greenery. The beach's name is said to be derived from seven Japanese commandos who were stranded there during World War II. Today, it offers a serene escape with activities like snorkeling, kayaking, and cliff diving, all framed by towering limestone cliffs and swaying coconut trees. The beach is part of the popular El Nido Island Hopping Tour A, making it a must-visit for those exploring the island's natural beauty and vibrant marine life.
