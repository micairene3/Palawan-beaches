Activities in Malcapuya Beach

Snorkeling

Beach snorkeling is a fun and accessible way to explore the underwater world. It allows you to swim along the surface of the water with a mask.

Swimming

The calm waters are perfect for swimming, whether you're looking to relax or take a refreshing dip.

Hiking

Hiking is the best for those who enjoy a bit of adventure, there are hiking trails that lead to elevated viewpoints, offering panoramic vistas of the island and surrounding waters.

Photography

Beach photography is a genre that captures the beauty of coastal landscapes, people, and wildlife, focusing on the unique lighting and composition challenges presented by beach environments.

Island Hopping

Island hopping is a popular travel activity that involves visiting multiple islands, typically by boat, to explore beaches, lagoons, and marine life.

Stargazing

Stargazing is the practice of observing celestial objects in the night sky, from stars and planets to meteors and galaxies, often combining science, art, and wonder.

Sunbathing

Sunbathing is the act of sitting or lying in the sun, often to relax or tan, while gaining potential health benefits from sunlight exposure.

Diving

Beach diving, also known as shore diving, is scuba diving that begins directly from a beach or shoreline rather than from a boat, offering unique accessibility and self-guided experiences.

Famous Foods in Malcapuya Beach

Buko Juice

Buko juice a refreshing drink that made from a mixture of young coconut water, young coconut meat, and milk. This juice is rich in electrolytes and minerals.


Tamilok

Tamilok referred to as a woodworm or woodworm mollusk. It is found in decaying mangrove logs and is typically consumed as a raw fish dish, often served ceviche-style with coconut vinegar and chili.


Grilled Seafood

Seafood grill refers to the practice of cooking seafood using a grill, which involves applying heat to the food directly from above. Grilling seafood enhances its natural flavors, often resulting in a smoky and charred taste


Turon

Turon, also known as banana lumpia, is a popular Filipino snack made by wrapping ripe saba bananas in spring roll wrappers and deep-frying them until golden brown. It is often coated with caramelized sugar, giving it a sweet and crispy exterior.
