Activities in Hidden Beach

Camping

Camping is a form of outdoor recreation or outdoor education involving overnight stays with a basic temporary shelter such as a tent.

Fishing

Fishing is the activity of trying to catch fish. Fish are often caught as wildlife from the natural environment (freshwater or marine), but may also be caught from stocked bodies of water such as rivers and seas.

Swimming

Swimming is the art and skill of moving through water using coordinated body movements, typically involving the arms, legs, and core. It can be enjoyed as a recreational activity, a competitive sport, or a lifesaving skill.

Snorkeling

Beach snorkeling is a fun and accessible way to explore the underwater world. It allows you to swim along the surface of the water with a mask.

Island Hopping

Island hopping is a popular travel activity that involves visiting multiple islands, typically by boat, to explore beaches, lagoons, and marine life.

Kayaking

Beach kayaking is a thrilling and exhilarating way to enjoy the ocean's beauty and experience the thrill of paddling.

Diving

Beach diving, also known as shore diving, is scuba diving that begins directly from a beach or shoreline rather than from a boat, offering unique accessibility and self-guided experiences.

Cultural Tours

Cultural tours in the beach context refer to travel experiences that combine cultural immersion with beach relaxation.

Famous Foods in Hidden Beach

Garlic Shrimp

Garlic shrimp is a flavorful seafood dish made by sautéing shrimp in garlic-infused oil or butter, often enhanced with lemon and herbs.


Pork Sisig

Pork Sisig is a popular Filipino dish, known for its unique flavor profile that combines savory, tangy, spicy, and smoky notes.


Pancit

Pancit is a traditional Filipino noodle dish, often stir-fried with meat, vegetables, and a savory sauce, and is a staple in Filipino cuisine.


Halo-Halo

Halo-halo, formally spelled haluhalo, is considered the unofficial national dessert of the Philippines. Its name means "mix-mix" in Filipino, reflecting the way the dessert is eaten—by mixing all the ingredients together before enjoying it.
