Sabang Beach

Sabang Beach is a local culture and broader history of Palawan. This area was primarily inhabited by the Tagbanwa and Batak tribes, who had deep spiritual and historical connections to the Palawan landscape, including the karst limestone formations that back the beach and form the nearby underground river. As Palawan opened up to tourism, Sabang Beach emerged as a popular destination, balancing development with a strong commitment to environmental preservation. The beach's efforts to maintain its natural charm while welcoming visitors embody sustainable practices that protect Palawan's heritage.
