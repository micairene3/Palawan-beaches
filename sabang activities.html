Activities in Sabang Beach

Island Hopping

Island hopping refers to the travel practice of visiting multiple islands within an archipelago or group of islands during a single trip.

Beach Diving

Beach diving, also known as shore diving, is a form of scuba diving where divers enter the water directly from a shoreline or beach.

Kayaking

Kayaking is the use of a kayak for moving over water, distinguished by the sitting position of the paddler.

Snorkeling

Snorkeling is the practice of swimming face-down in a body of water while breathing through a snorkel, which is a shaped tube, usually accompanied by swimming goggles or a diving mask and swim fins.

Ziplining

Ziplining is an outdoor activity that involves gliding along a suspended cable while harnessed to a pulley system, often at high speeds.

Hiking

Hiking is a rewarding outdoor activity that allows you to explore nature, improve your fitness, and enjoy the great outdoors.

Beach Volleyball

Beach volleyball is a fast-paced, team-based sport played on sand, usually with two players per side, emphasizing teamwork, skill, and agility.

Surfing

Surfing is a surface water sport where an individual, known as a surfer, uses a board to ride on the forward section of a moving wave, typically towards the shore.

Foods in Sabang Beach

Sisig

A sizzling dish made from chopped pork, onions, chili, and seasoning served on a hot plate. It is very popular in beach bars and is often eaten with drinks.


Adobo

Famous Filipino dishes. It is meat (usually pork or chicken) cooked in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, and spices. It has a rich savory flavor and is commonly served with rice.


Kare-Kare

A traditional Filipino stew made with peanut sauce, vegetables, and beef or pork. It is usually served with bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) for extra flavor.


BBQ and Grilled Food

Beach restaurants also serve grilled pork BBQ, chicken inasal, and skewers, which are perfect for casual beach dining.
